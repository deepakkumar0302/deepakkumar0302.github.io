Deepak Kumar

PhD Candidate in Agricultural & Applied Economics · Virginia Tech

I am an applied microeconomist studying how climate shocks, refugee settlements, and agricultural technologies shape economic outcomes in developing countries. I combine high-frequency energy use data, satellite nightlights, and household surveys to measure local economic activity and inform policy.

Research interests

  • Economic impacts of climate shocks and natural disasters.
  • Refugee-host interactions and local economy-wide effects.
  • Adoption of agricultural and climate-smart technologies by smallholder farmers.
  • Impact evaluation and applied microeconometrics in low- and middle-income countries.
